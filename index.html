Why it exists

Not a quality score. Not a copyright claim. Not AI detection.

Existing disclosure frameworks are built for academic manuscripts, demand paragraph-length statements, and stop at the edge of plain text. GENMARK is built for everything else — a photo, a product label, a podcast description, a line of code.

What it does: declares, in two characters, where an idea originated and how much a human shaped the final result — on the artifact itself, at a glance.

What it doesn’t do: rank the work, assert ownership, or detect anything. It runs on the same honor system as © and ™ — a claim the maker chooses to make.

The system

Two axes. Nine combinations. Two exceptions.

Every code reads left to right: origin of the idea, then degree of human oversight on the output.

Conception ↓ / Curation → 0 — Unreviewed 1 — Human-checked 2 — Human-refined
H — Human (G)H0Human prompts; first AI output published as-is. (G)H1Human briefs, AI drafts, human approves with minor edits. (G)H2Author writes every word; AI only checks grammar.
C — Collaborative —Not defined — collaborative origin implies some curation. (G)C1Iterative prompting; AI draft published with light selection. (G)C2Iterative back-and-forth; human substantially revises.
A — AI (G)A0Generic prompt; AI determines everything; first output used. (G)A1AI generates many outputs; human selects one. (G)A2AI produces the first draft; human substantially transforms it.

(G)HH Full human authorship, no AI involved at any stage.

Where it applies

On the artifact when it matters. In the metadata when it doesn’t.

The public mark applies when human authorship is the default assumption, and knowing otherwise would change how someone weighs the work. Decorative or operational content skips the public mark and goes straight to internal records.

Displayed on the work

  • Footer or colophon
  • Image caption or credit line
  • Video description field
  • Print margin or back matter
  • Product label or packaging
  • Slide deck footer

Machine-readable

  • genmark: H2 in metadata
  • EXIF field for images
  • HTML meta tag
  • JSON-LD structured data
  • File header comment
  • Asset provenance field

Written citation

  • “GENMARK H2” in a bibliography
  • Acknowledgments section
  • Author note in an academic paper
  • Press release disclosure
